\begin{document}

\begin{claims}

\begin{claim}
A piezoresistive pressure sensor comprising:
a sense bridge \refnum{10} of piezoresistors on a sensing diaphragm;
a split-path excitation network \refnum{40} supplying the sense bridge and
comprising a constant-current leg and a proportional-to-absolute-temperature
leg whose relative contribution is set by a single ratio resistor \refnum{12},
the proportional-to-absolute-temperature leg increasing bridge excitation with
temperature so as to oppose a negative temperature coefficient of sensitivity of
the sense bridge; and
a dummy half-bridge \refnum{20} fabricated adjacent to the sense bridge and
exposed to a common die temperature and process corner, a midpoint of the dummy
half-bridge being summed into an output of the sense bridge so as to cancel a
temperature-dependent offset of the sense bridge.
\end{claim}

\begin{claim}
The sensor of claim 1, further comprising a span trim resistor \refnum{30}
setting an output gain and trimmed at a single temperature, wherein the gain set
at said single temperature holds across an operating temperature range by virtue
of the split-path excitation network.
\end{claim}

\begin{claim}
The sensor of claim 1, wherein the dummy half-bridge \refnum{20} and the sense
bridge \refnum{10} are integrated on a common MEMS die using a common
piezoresistor implant, the dummy half-bridge being located outside a flexing
region of the sensing diaphragm.
\end{claim}

\end{claims}
